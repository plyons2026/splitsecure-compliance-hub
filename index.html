Threat landscape
Why third-party privileged access risk is the defining compliance challenge of 2026
The threat landscape has shifted from perimeter attacks to supply chain exploitation. Attackers no longer need to breach an enterprise directly — they target the vendors with standing privileged access to thousands of customer environments simultaneously.

Evolving threat vectors
Now
Vendor-hosted PAM as supply chain attack surface
Most PAM solutions store complete credentials on behalf of customers. A breach of the PAM vendor simultaneously exposes every customer's privileged credentials. One perimeter breach yields many.
Active
Now
Bearer token replay and post-authentication exploitation
Session tokens, API keys, and SSH certificates are bearer instruments. MFA protects the login event. It does not protect what a stolen token can do after the fact.
Active
Emerging
Agentic systems with unchecked privileged access
Agentic AI systems inherit whatever credentials they are given and act on them without human oversight. Standing privileged credentials become an autonomous attack surface.
Emerging
2026–30
Harvest now, decrypt later — quantum exploitation of stored secrets
Nation-state adversaries are collecting encrypted credentials today to decrypt when quantum computers become capable. NIST and NSA estimate Q-Day could arrive as early as 2030. Stored secrets are already at risk.
Near-term
Why SplitSecure addresses all four vectors
No stored credential to target
Shamir Secret Sharing splits every credential across independent devices. No single device, vendor, or operator holds a complete credential. There is no vault to breach.
No token sufficient to act unilaterally
Reconstruction requires a live quorum. A stolen bearer token cannot authorize privileged action. Possession no longer equals authority.
Agentic systems cannot act alone
No agentic system can reconstruct a credential unilaterally. Every privileged action requires independent human quorum approval regardless of the initiating system.
Nothing to harvest for quantum decryption
SplitSecure eliminates persistent secrets. There is no stored credential for a quantum system to target. Harvest now, decrypt later attacks have nothing to collect.
Regulatory frameworks
The shift from policy to mechanism — what regulators now require
For years, regulators accepted documented policies as sufficient evidence of controls. The 2026 compliance landscape marks a structural shift. Regulators now distinguish between a policy, which can be ignored or overridden, and a mechanism, which is architecturally enforced. Vendor-hosted PAM satisfies the former. SplitSecure satisfies the latter.
Framework requirements and SplitSecure mapping
NYDFS Part 500
Access governance
Requires PAM implementation and limits privileged accounts to strictly necessary functions. Third-party access must be governed by technical constraint. No single party should hold unilateral authority.

Proof of compliance
Architectural proof, not self-reported attestation
SplitSecure produces continuous, cryptographically signed evidence that privileged access controls are enforced. Not assembled for audit. Generated by the mechanism itself. Every record is immutable, bound to the approver identities and runtime, and independently verifiable.
How SplitSecure generates compliance evidence
01
No complete credential is ever stored — architecturally verified
Every credential is split using Shamir Secret Sharing across independent devices. Deployment attestation confirms no single device holds a complete credential. This is not a contractual promise — it is cryptographically enforced and independently verifiable.
Evidence: Shamir Secret Sharing deployment attestation, shard distribution record

02
Every privileged action requires quorum approval — logged immutably
No single party can execute a privileged action unilaterally. Every action produces a cryptographically signed, time-stamped record bound to the approver identities and attested runtime. The record cannot be altered after the fact because it is a byproduct of the enforcement mechanism.
Evidence: quorum approval log, zero single-approver actions
Verifiable
03
Production and recovery credentials are governed independently
Production systems and recovery systems require separate, independent quorum groups. A compromise of production credentials cannot reach recovery systems. The architectural condition that caused the CloudNordic total loss is impossible in a SplitSecure-protected environment.
Evidence: quorum group independence attestation, separation of duties record
